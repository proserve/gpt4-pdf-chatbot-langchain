Lord Bingham, who passed away in 2010, was a highly respected British jurist and legal scholar who served as a Lord of Appeal in Ordinary in the UK House of Lords. He was a strong advocate for the rule of law, which he described as one of the fundamental principles of modern democratic society.Lord Bingham believed that the rule of law requires that all individuals and entities, including the government, be subject to the law, and that the law should be applied fairly and impartially to everyone. He also emphasised the importance of access to justice and the need for legal remedies to be available to all who seek them.In addition, Lord Bingham stressed the importance of human rights and the need to protect them through the law. He believed that the law should provide a framework for protecting individual freedoms, and that it should be interpreted in light of these fundamental rights.Overall, Lord Bingham's work and advocacy for the rule of law have had a significant impact on legal theory and practice in the UK and beyond.
